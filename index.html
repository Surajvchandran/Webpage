Cyber Security Basics

Understanding threats, protecting your digital life

What is Cyber Security?

Cyber security is the discipline of protecting systems, networks, programs, and data from digital attacks. These attacks typically aim to access, alter, or destroy sensitive information; extort money; or interrupt normal business operations.

Cybersecurity involves a broad range of tools, strategies, and practices, including firewalls, antivirus software, secure coding, user awareness, and incident response plans. Effective cybersecurity reduces the risk of cyberattacks and protects organizations and individuals from the unauthorized exploitation of systems, networks, and technologies.

As our digital footprint expands, cybersecurity has become a fundamental part of our everyday lives, from online banking and shopping to health monitoring and smart home devices.


Common Threats

  • Malware: Malicious software such as viruses, worms, spyware, and trojans that infiltrate and damage devices or steal data.

  • Phishing: Deceptive attempts to acquire sensitive information (like passwords or credit card details) by posing as trustworthy entities via email or fake websites.

  • Ransomware: A type of malware that encrypts a victim's data and demands a ransom payment to restore access.

  • Man-in-the-middle attacks: An attacker intercepts and possibly alters the communication between two parties without their knowledge, often to steal data.

  • Social Engineering: Manipulating individuals into divulging confidential information by exploiting human psychology.

  • Zero-Day Attacks: Targeting vulnerabilities in software that are unknown to the vendor and unpatched.

  • Insider Threats: Threats that originate from within the organization, such as employees or contractors with access to sensitive information.

  • Denial-of-Service (DoS/DDoS) Attacks: Overwhelming a system or network to make it inaccessible to users, often disrupting operations.

Basic Protection Steps

  • Use Strong Passwords: Create complex, unique passwords and store them securely using a password manager. Avoid using the same password across multiple accounts.

  • Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA): Adds a second layer of protection by requiring a one-time code or device confirmation in addition to your password.

  • Keep Software Updated: Regularly update operating systems, apps, and security tools to patch vulnerabilities and enhance protection.

  • Backup Your Data: Make frequent backups of important files to secure cloud storage or offline drives, so you can restore your data if it’s lost or encrypted by ransomware.

  • Be Cautious with Links and Attachments: Avoid clicking suspicious links or downloading unknown attachments, especially from untrusted sources.

  • Use Antivirus & Firewall: Install reputable antivirus software and activate your system’s firewall to monitor and block malicious traffic.

  • Limit Personal Data Sharing: Minimize the information you share online and on social media, which attackers can use for social engineering.

  • Educate Yourself & Others: Stay informed about emerging cyber threats and share knowledge with peers to build a stronger defense network.
